What is AADHAAR?

Aadhaar is a 12 digit <UID>(unique identity) number that can be obtained by residents of India

It is a digital ID issued, by the Unique Identification Authority of India(UIDAI), under the supervision of the Government of India, under the Ministry of Electronics and IT.
It also consists of the Aadhaar Auth API. The process wherein Aadhaar Number, along with other attributes, including biometrics, are submitted online to the CIDR for verification on the basis of information or data or documents available with it.

What is in Aadhaar?

The AADHAAR data is stored in about 7000 servers in Bengaluru and Manesar, Haryana. They are well guarded and protected. They also contain the following dat about registrants.

  • Name
  • Gender
  • Address
  • Date of Birth
  • Ten Fingerprint scans
  • Two Iris Scans
  • Photograph

    • Features

    • → Single Verification

      Single source of Identity verification which means less paper-work and more work done.

    • Links

      Everything from bank acc. to passport and driving licence is connected together.

    • Time Efficiency

      The time required to verify and do the paper work is saved. This leads to better efficiency.

    • Biometric Security

      Use of Finger print and of multiple biometrics in validation before granting access to data. These include among others, eye-iris scanning.

    Judiciary speaks

    The Hon. Supreme Court of India has given a verdict.

    • The Govt Cannot Make AADHAAR mandatory for availing Govt Services.

      It also said that the collection, usage and storage of Citizens’ biometric is a violation of individual privacy. This drew a lot of question on Aadhaar policy. It took a long legal battle until the September 2018, for SC to say that AADHAAR was constitutionally valid.

    • The SC has notably squashed the multi-utility of the Aadhaar card.

      Saying that it is not at all mandatory for taking admissions in institutions, issue of new sim cards and for the use of CBSE. Another major controversy was regarding, the linking process where Aadhaar was supposed to be linked to PAN, phone number, etc. There was no vision or a significant justification on why this move was initiated and also regarding the present status of the same by the govt.

    What can you do if your Aadhar is stolen...

    • Section 47 relates to allowing individuals to file complaints against the use of their data and section 57 to private entities demanding Aadhaar data to authenticate identity.
    • Section 33(2), related to information disclosure for matters of national security has been struck down in its present form, but exceptions can be made, and to prevent misuse, a judicial officer such as a sitting High Court Judge must sanction it.

    Data Safety

    Data packets are stored behind 2048-bit encryption and capable of self-destruction if any unauthorized access is attempted.

    • UIDAI does not reveal personal information in the Aadhaar database – the only response is a ‘yes’ or ‘no’ to requests to verify an identity.
    • Some experts, however, say that ultimately every system is prone to some or other risk of error or mischief, and Aadhaar is no exception. Hence, be it a government agency or a third party managing it, there will always be a risk of human error or mischief.
